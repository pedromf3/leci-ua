Summer

Allegro non molto

Under a hard season, fired up by the sun
Languishes man, languishes the flock and burns the pine We hear the cuckoo's voice;
then sweet songs of the turtledove and finch are heard.
Soft breezes stir the air, but threatening the North Wind1 sweeps them suddenly aside.
The shepherd trembles, fearing violent storms and his fate.

Adagio e piano – Presto e forte

The fear of lightning and fierce thunder
Robs his tired limbs of rest2
As gnats and flies buzz furiously around.

Presto

Alas, his fears were justified
The Heavens thunder and roar and with hail
Cut the head off the wheat and damages the grain.
